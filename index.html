Scam Awareness

Know how to spot and avoid the most common scams targeting Filipinos online.

🎣

Phishing Sites

Fake websites mimicking real ones to steal credentials. Always check the URL — scammers use tricky domains like amazoncom-mytv.com instead of amazon.com.

💼

Fake Job Offers

Too-good-to-be-true work-from-home offers promising ₱500–₱2,000/day. They eventually ask for a "registration fee" or your personal info.

💸

GCash / Maya Scams

Fake "you received money" notifications with phishing links. Always open GCash/Maya directly from the app — never click links in SMS or chat.

❤️

Romance Scams

Scammers build fake relationships then ask for money. If someone online asks for money, it's almost always a scam.

💻

Tech Support Scams

"Your computer has a virus! Call Microsoft now!" — Microsoft, Google, and Apple will never call you unsolicited.

🏦

Investment Pyramiding

Very common in Mindanao. If returns depend on recruitment, it's pyramiding — illegal and unsustainable.

About ScamBuster PH

ScamBuster PH started from a single scambaiting session — finding a network of 8 phishing sites impersonating Amazon, doing full WHOIS recon, and reporting them to Amazon Trust & Safety, MarkMonitor, Google Safe Browsing, and Netcraft.

Within hours, Netcraft confirmed the primary URL as malicious. Amazon Security personally responded and confirmed they are investigating. That's when ScamBuster PH became a real thing.

The mission is simple: find scams targeting Filipinos, document everything, report them to the right people, and make sure they get taken down.
